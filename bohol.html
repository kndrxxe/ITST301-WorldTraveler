BOHOL

Bohol is an island province located in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. It is located to the southeast of Cebu and is known for its natural beauty, including the Chocolate Hills, a series of small hills that are covered in green grass and turn brown during the dry season, giving them their name. The province is also home to a number of other natural attractions, such as the Loboc River and the Panglao Island beach resort. Bohol is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species, including the Philippine tarsier, a small primate that is native to the island. The province is also known for its historical and cultural sites, including the Baclayon Church, which dates back to the 16th century.

ITINERARY

Bohol is a popular tourist destination, with many visitors coming to see the Chocolate Hills and other natural attractions, as well as to enjoy the island's beautiful beaches and rich cultural history. Here is a sample tour itinerary for Bohol:

DAY 1

  • Arrival in Bohol and transfer to the hotel
  • Lunch at a local restaurant
  • Visit the Chocolate Hills and take a picture at the viewing deck
  • Visit the Tarsier Conservation Area to see the Philippine tarsiers
  • Dinner at a local restaurant

DAY 2

  • Breakfast at the hotel
  • Take a boat ride along the Loboc River and enjoy a buffet lunch on a floating restaurant
  • Visit the Baclayon Church, a historical church dating back to the 16th century
  • Stop at the Blood Compact Shrine, a monument to the first treaty of friendship between the Philippines and Spain
  • Visit the Hanging Bridge, a popular tourist attraction in the town of Sevilla
  • Dinner at a local restaurant

DAY 3

  • Breakfast at the hotel
  • Visit the Panglao Island beach resort for swimming and relaxation
  • Lunch at a beachside restaurant
  • Visit the Dauis Church, a historical church with a museum showcasing old artifacts
  • Dinner at a local restaurant

DAY 4

  • Breakfast at the hotel
  • Transfer to the airport for departure
